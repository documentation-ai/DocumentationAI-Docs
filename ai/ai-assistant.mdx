## Overview

The AI Assistant helps readers, customers, and internal teams get fast, accurate answers based on your published documentation. When someone clicks **Ask AI**, the assistant searches your docs, reasons over the content it finds, and returns grounded answers with citations.

Unlike a generic chatbot, the assistant is:

- **Docs‑aware** – it relies on your published documentation as its source of truth.

- **Agentic** – it plans how to answer, not just reply turn‑by‑turn.

- **Cited** – it shows where each answer came from in your docs.

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  The AI Assistant (Ask AI) is separate from the AI Documentation Agent. The **Assistant** answers questions for end users and internal teams.
  The **Documentation Agent** helps you write and edit content. See [AI Documentation Agent](/ai/ai-documentation-agent) for details.
</Callout>

## More than just AI Search – it’s Agentic

Most chatbots:

- Don’t know your docs structure

- Freely improvise when they’re unsure

- Make it hard to see where an answer came from

The AI Assistant:

- **Retrieves first, then answers** – it searches your docs and uses the retrieved content as grounding.

- **Plans actions, not just replies** – it reasons about which docs to consult, when to ask follow‑up questions, and how to structure multi‑step answers.

- **Stays within your content** – it is designed to prefer your docs over general model knowledge.

- **Signals uncertainty** – when it can’t find reliable content, it avoids making confident but unsupported claims.

This makes it safer to use in support and customer‑facing workflows.

## Citations: how they work and why they matter

Every answer from the assistant can include **citations** back to your documentation.

What users see:

- A short answer and explanation in the chat

- One or more citations linked to specific pages (often down to the section)

- The ability to click through to read the full context

Why this is important:

- **Trust** – users can verify information against your canonical docs.

- **Reusability** – support and account teams can safely reuse answers with links in tickets, chats, or emails.

- **Maintainability** – updating your docs improves future answers automatically; you can trace bad answers back to specific content.

If an answer cannot be strongly supported by what’s in your docs, the assistant will either:

- Ask for clarification, or

- Indicate that it doesn’t have enough information, instead of fabricating details.

## Where users access the assistant

The assistant appears in your documentation as an **Ask AI** entry point. Depending on your configuration, it can open as a full assistant panel or as a floating chat bubble.

1. Visitors open **Ask AI**, or land on a page where the assistant opens automatically if **default open** is enabled.

2. Before they ask a question, they can click one of your configured **conversation starters** to begin with a common task or question.

3. The assistant searches your docs, reasons over the results, and responds with:

   - A concise answer or explanation

   - Optional step‑by‑step guidance

   - Citations and links to relevant pages or sections

   - Suggested follow‑up questions when the assistant has relevant next questions to recommend

<Callout kind="alert" collapsed="false">
  Currently, the AI Assistant is enabled for all documentation sites. If you would like to disable it, please contact us.
</Callout>

## Configure the assistant experience

Use the `ai` section of `documentation.json` to control how the assistant appears for readers.

```json
{
  "ai": {
    "ai-assistant:enabled": true,
    "ai-assistant:style": "floating",
    "ai-assistant:position": "bottom-spaced",
    "ai-assistant:default-open": false,
    "ai-assistant:starter-questions": [
      "How do I set up a custom domain?",
      "How do I import my OpenAPI file?"
    ]
  }
}
```

<ParamField body="ai-assistant:enabled" param-type="boolean" required="false">
Turns the AI Assistant on or off for your documentation site.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="ai-assistant:style" param-type="string" required="false">
Controls how the assistant is presented in your docs. Set this to `floating` to show the assistant as a floating chat bubble. Other supported styles continue to render in the standard assistant panel layout for your site.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="ai-assistant:position" param-type="string" required="false">
Controls the vertical placement of the floating assistant. Use `default` for the standard bottom offset, or `bottom-spaced` to raise the bubble higher above the bottom edge. This setting only applies when `ai-assistant:style` is set to `floating`.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="ai-assistant:default-open" param-type="boolean" required="false">
Opens the assistant automatically when a visitor lands on your documentation. Leave this disabled if you want readers to start the conversation manually.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="ai-assistant:starter-questions" param-type="string[]" required="false">
Defines the suggested questions shown before the first message. Use these to highlight common workflows, onboarding questions, or high‑value support topics.
</ParamField>

Follow‑up suggestions are shown after an answer when the assistant has helpful next questions to recommend. This gives readers a faster way to continue the conversation without retyping context.

<Callout kind="info" collapsed="false">
  If you previously configured the assistant with `ai-assistant:mode`, rename that key to `ai-assistant:style`. Keep the same style value you want to use, and add `ai-assistant:position` only when you are using the floating assistant.
</Callout>

## Benefits for admins and doc owners

The AI Assistant can:

- **Improve onboarding** – new users reach first value faster by asking concrete “how do I…” questions.

- **Reduce support tickets** – many basic “where is…” and “how do I…” questions are handled directly in the docs.

- **Make customer‑facing teams faster** – support and AM/CS teams can use the same assistant to pull accurate, cited answers during conversations.

- **Expose documentation gaps** – repeated questions without good citations highlight where your docs need better coverage or structure.
